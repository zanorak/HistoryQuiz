U.S. History Quiz

Albany Plan of Union

1754 - Seven of the British colonies made a plan to create a central government. It did not work.

French and Indian War

1754-1763 - The British and French fought over the new world and this led to the Seven Years War.

Proclamation of 1763

1763 - The British government blocked expansion into the western territories fearing further conflict with the French.

Sons of Liberty

1765 - A loosely organized group that fought against taxation from the British. They used “No Taxation without Representation” as a motto.

Stamp Act

1765-1766 - The British enacted the stamp act to recoup costs of the French and Indian war. This led to refusal to use the stamps and rioting and harassment of stamp agents.

Boston Massacre

1770 - Hundreds of protesting colonists were fired upon by British soldiers, killing 3. This event helped enrage the people leading up the the revolution.

Committees of Correspondence

1773-1775 - Committees of correspondence were emergency provisional governments set up in the 13 American colonies.

Boston Tea Party

1773 - Massachusetts colonists disguised as Indians dumped tea into the Boston harbor. This was in protest of the various taxes that the British had imposed to help pay for the French and Indian War.

Coercive/Intolerable Acts

1774 - In response to the Boston Tea Party, the British passed 4 laws to punish the colonists. They covered repayment for the tea, import and export of goods, The appointment of council members and judges and the quartering of soldiers.

Battle of Lexington and Concord

1775 - Battles that started the Revolutionary War. This was the Ride of Paul Revere, Lexington Green, The Shot Heard Around the World, Minutemen. It was the culmination of tensions that had been increasing for years.

Common Sense

1776 - Thomas Paine appealed to the common people for support in gaining independence from Great Britain.

Draft of the Declaration of Independence

1777 - Thomas Jefferson finishes the draft of the Declaration of Independence. It included the end of slavery but this was removed in order to get support of the southern colonies.

Second Continental Congress

1774-1781 - Representatives from the thirteen colonies met to run the war. It was this group that wrote and approved the Declaration of Independence.
